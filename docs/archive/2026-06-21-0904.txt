2. HAMLOCK HARDWARE BACKDOOR
   Vulnerabilities and Exploits · [hardware, backdoor,
   supply-chain]
   Latest developments: Researchers at the University of
   Tennessee and the University of Florida unveiled HAMLOCK, a
   backdoor that splits its malicious logic across the FPGA and
   ASIC silicon that edge devices use to run neural networks.
   Edge AI systems often lean on third-party-designed FPGAs and
   ASICs for speed and efficiency, opening a supply-chain risk.
   HAMLOCK divides its function between hardware blocks so
   neither half looks malicious on its own, hiding the implant
   in plain sight.
   - Help Net Security: https://www.helpnetsecurity.com/2026/06/21/week-in-review-74k-fortinet-firewall-credentials-stolen-splunk-enterprise-rce-under-active-attack/
