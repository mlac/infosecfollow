2. PHANTOM SQUATTING
   AI Security · [ai, supply-chain, phishing]
   Latest developments: Palo Alto Networks' Unit 42 published
   research July 1 naming the technique phantom squatting, in
   which attackers register the nonexistent web addresses large
   language models invent, then host phishing pages and malware
   on them to catch the traffic AI tools point their way.
   Large language models routinely hallucinate package and
   domain names that do not exist, and attackers buy those
   addresses first to weaponize them against software supply
   chains and everyday users. Developers should verify every
   AI-suggested dependency and domain before trusting it.
   - Unit 42 (Palo Alto): https://unit42.paloaltonetworks.com/phantom-squatting-hallucinated-web-domains/
   - The Hacker News: https://thehackernews.com/2026/07/phantom-squatting-uses-ai-hallucinated.html
