2. BIOSHOCKING AND AGENTJACKING TURN AI ASSISTANTS ON THEIR
   USERS
   AI Security · [ai, prompt-injection, vulnerability]
   Latest developments: LayerX disclosed BioShocking on June 30,
   a technique that convinced six AI browsers—including OpenAI's
   ChatGPT Atlas, Perplexity's Comet, and Anthropic's Claude
   browser extension—that risky actions were fiction, prompting
   them to copy a user's login details and send them to an
   attacker, while Dark Reading detailed Agentjacking, a fake
   bug report that hijacks AI coding agents at scale.
   Both attacks exploit an AI's inability to separate content
   from instructions: telling a model that 2 plus 2 equals 5, or
   planting instructions in a bug report, makes it ignore its
   guardrails. Organizations deploying AI browsers and coding
   agents should tightly limit what those agents can read,
   reach, and act on.
   - The Hacker News: https://thehackernews.com/2026/06/new-bioshocking-attack-tricks-ai.html
   - Ars Technica Security: https://arstechnica.com/security/2026/06/ai-browsers-can-be-lulled-into-a-dream-world-where-guardrails-no-longer-apply/
   - Dark Reading: https://www.darkreading.com/cyber-risk/fake-bug-report-hijacks-ai-coding-agents
   - BleepingComputer: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/new-bioshocking-attack-manipulates-ai-browser-into-data-theft/
